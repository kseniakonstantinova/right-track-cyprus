# Site Design and Content Guide

## 1. Purpose

This guide translates the Right Track Physiotherapy Brand Book into practical rules for building and maintaining:
- website pages (EN and EL)
- presentation decks and slide materials
- campaign assets and promotional content

**Who this is for:** any agent, developer, or designer producing pages, decks, or assets for Right Track.

**What it governs:** visual language, content tone, page structure, CTA hierarchy, card/section systems, badge usage, typography, color application, imagery, and graphic elements.

**Relationship to SEO guidelines:** this guide defines *how things look and sound*. The SEO guidelines (`docs/seo-guidelines.md`) define *how things are structured for search*. Both must be followed together. Where this guide mentions page structure (H1, hero, metadata), the SEO guidelines are authoritative. Where the SEO guidelines are silent on visual treatment, this guide governs.

---

## 2. Brand Translation

### What "The Athlete's Physio" means in practice

Right Track is a performance-focused rehabilitation clinic. Not a wellness studio. Not a hospital. A performance facility where athletes recover, train, and return to sport.

Every page must communicate:
- **Authority** — we are clinical experts with real credentials
- **Athletic energy** — we operate at the pace of sport, not the pace of a waiting room
- **Results orientation** — every service leads to measurable performance outcomes
- **Comeback narrative** — injury is a detour, not a destination

### How to write like Right Track

The brand voice is **coach + expert**: a coach who happens to be a medical expert, not a doctor trying to sound like an athlete.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Get back to full performance" | "Feel better soon" |
| "We build your return-to-sport plan" | "We offer a variety of wellness services" |
| "Your recovery is training" | "Take it easy and rest" |
| "Evidence-based treatment for athletes" | "Holistic healing for body and mind" |
| "Book your assessment" | "Enquire about our services" |

**Expert without cold:** Use plain language. Explain clinical concepts in 1-2 sentences. Cite qualifications where they build trust (GESY registration, Frederick University supervision). Don't hide behind jargon.

**Motivational without generic:** Anchor motivation in specifics. "Return to the pitch in 8 weeks" is motivational. "Live your best life" is generic. Every motivational statement should imply a concrete outcome.

### Tone by context

| Context | Tone adjustment |
|---|---|
| Service page | Confident, clinical authority + clear outcomes |
| Blog article | Educational, coach-like, specific |
| Community/partnership page | Collaborative, professional, partner-readable |
| Seminar/resource page | Expert educator, generous with knowledge |
| Campaign/promo | Direct, urgent, outcome-focused |
| Profile page | Personal credibility, human warmth, athletic identity |

## 3. Naming and Messaging Rules

### Brand name usage
- **Full:** Right Track Physiotherapy (first mention on any page)
- **Short:** Right Track (subsequent references)
- **Tagline:** The Athlete's Physio
- **Never:** RT Physio, Right Track PT, RightTrack

### Page titles
- Format: `Primary Intent | Right Track Physiotherapy Cyprus`
- Lead with the user's search intent, not the brand name
- Include location modifier only where it supports search intent (services, homepage)
- Do not keyword-stuff

### Visible headings (H1)
- One H1 per page (SEO requirement)
- Must reflect the primary intent of the page
- Use orange `<span>` highlight on the key differentiating word
- Keep under 10 words

### Metadata descriptions
- Unique per page
- Reflect visible page purpose
- Include one outcome-oriented phrase
- 120-155 characters

### CTA copy rules
- Use action verbs: Book, Download, View, Start, Get
- Be specific: "Book Your Assessment" not "Learn More"
- Match CTA text to page intent (see Section 6)
- Never use "Submit" or "Click Here"

### Profile/about text
- Lead with credentials and sport focus
- Include GESY registration number
- Reference specific sports or conditions treated
- Avoid generic bio language ("passionate about helping people")

### Partner-facing content
- Use "collaboration" not "sponsorship"
- Name the partner organization explicitly
- Describe mutual value clearly
- Include Right Track's role and contribution

## 4. Page Families

### Homepage
- **Primary intent:** Establish Right Track as the athlete's physio in Nicosia
- **Primary CTA:** Book Online (orange)
- **Above the fold:** H1 with location, tagline, dual CTA (Book + secondary), trust signals (stats/credentials)
- **Secondary:** Services overview, team preview, testimonials, blog highlights
- **Tone:** Confident, welcoming to athletes, establishes credibility immediately

### Service page
- **Primary intent:** Explain one service and convert to booking
- **Primary CTA:** Book Your Assessment / Book This Service
- **Above the fold:** Service name (H1), key benefit statement, GESY + price badges, hero CTA
- **Secondary:** Conditions treated grid, process steps, pricing cards, therapist card, FAQ, related services
- **Tone:** Clinical authority balanced with accessible explanation

### Blog article
- **Primary intent:** Educate on a specific topic, build trust, link to services
- **Primary CTA:** Book / related service link
- **Above the fold:** Article title (H1), tag/category, author, date, read time
- **Secondary:** Article body, FAQ accordion, related articles, service CTA
- **Tone:** Educational coach — teach the reader something actionable

### Clinician / profile page
- **Primary intent:** Build trust in a specific therapist
- **Primary CTA:** Book with this therapist
- **Above the fold:** Photo, name, title, key credentials, specializations
- **Secondary:** Bio, qualifications, treatment philosophy, patient reviews
- **Tone:** Personal, credible, human but professional

### Community / partnership page
- **Primary intent:** Show Right Track's community engagement and invite collaboration
- **Primary CTA:** Contact for collaboration / Discuss partnership
- **Above the fold:** Community identity (H1), subtitle explaining the page purpose, event/partnership cards
- **Secondary:** Past events, partner logos, collaboration opportunities
- **Tone:** Collaborative, professional, partner-readable by external organizations

### Seminar / resource page
- **Primary intent:** Deliver educational value (before event: register; after event: access materials)
- **Primary CTA (upcoming):** Register / Reserve Your Spot
- **Primary CTA (completed):** Download Guide / View Materials
- **Above the fold:** Guide/seminar title, collaboration context (if partnered), resource/download CTA, optional status or context line
- **Secondary:** Content preview, speaker/expert info, related resources
- **Tone:** Expert educator, generous, builds authority through shared knowledge

### Campaign / promo page
- **Primary intent:** Convert on a specific offer
- **Primary CTA:** Claim Offer / Book Now
- **Above the fold:** Offer headline, value proposition, urgency element, CTA
- **Secondary:** Offer details, testimonials, FAQ
- **Tone:** Direct, urgent, outcome-focused, never desperate

## 5. Hero System

### General hero rules
- Every hero must communicate one clear intent within 3 seconds
- Navy gradient overlay on athletic imagery is the default hero treatment
- Text is always white on dark hero backgrounds
- H1 uses orange `<span>` on the key differentiating word
- Hero must not try to serve two intents (e.g., don't mix "register for event" and "download guide" in the same hero)

### Hero by page type

**Homepage hero**
- Two-column layout: text left, visual right
- H1 with location modifier and orange highlight
- Dual CTA: primary (Book Online) + secondary (explore)
- Trust signals visible (stats strip below or within hero)
- Background: athletic imagery or gym environment

**Service page hero**
- Full-width with semi-transparent background image (opacity ~0.35)
- Navy gradient overlay
- H1 centered or left-aligned with orange `<span>`
- Badge row: GESY badge (white/translucent) + price badge (orange/translucent)
- Single hero CTA: Book button (orange, with glow shadow)
- Subtitle: 1 sentence describing the outcome

**Blog article hero**
- Clean, minimal — no background image
- Category tag badge above H1
- H1 as article title
- Meta row: author, date, read time
- No CTA in hero (CTA appears in article body and footer)

**Community page hero**
- Navy gradient background (no image)
- H1 with orange `<span>`, centered
- Subtitle explaining page purpose (white 70% opacity)
- No CTA in hero (cards below are the navigation)

**Seminar/resource page hero — upcoming event**
- Gradient overlay: navy-to-orange diagonal
- Decorative radial gradient accent (orange, low opacity)
- Collaboration badge if partnered (translucent pill badge)
- H1 centered, max-width 800px
- Event detail pills: date, time, location (icon + text) — appropriate in hero for upcoming events
- Single CTA: Register / Reserve Your Spot

**Seminar/resource page hero — completed event turned resource**
- Hero must be guide-first, not event-first
- H1 is the resource/guide title, not the original event name
- Collaboration context in badge or subtitle
- Single CTA: Download Guide / View Materials
- Event details (date, location, original event context) move below the fold as contextual info — they are not hero-level content

**What must never dominate the hero:**
- Partner logos larger than Right Track branding
- Multiple competing CTAs
- Long paragraphs of text
- Decorative elements that obscure the message
- Generic stock imagery

### Handling completed seminars
When a seminar is past:
- Remove registration CTA
- Replace with "Download Guide" / "View Materials" CTA
- Update badge to "Completed Seminar" or "Free Resource"
- Keep the educational content — it becomes a permanent resource page
- Do not leave dead registration links

### Handling partner/community heroes
- Right Track brand must be visually dominant
- Partner name appears in collaboration badge or subtitle, not in H1
- Use "In collaboration with [Partner]" pattern
- Partner logo may appear but smaller than Right Track identity

## 6. CTA Hierarchy

### Three CTA levels

**Primary CTA**
- Style: `.btn-orange` or `.btn-book-hero` — solid orange `#FF6B35` background, white text, 50px border-radius, glow shadow
- Purpose: the one action the page exists to drive
- Rule: **one primary CTA per page intent** (may repeat at top and bottom if context differs, e.g., hero and footer CTA section)
- Hover: darker orange `#e55a2b`, -2px lift, stronger shadow

**Secondary CTA**
- Style: `.btn-secondary` — translucent/white background, glass effect, navy or dark text
- Purpose: important alternative action (e.g., "View Services" on homepage, "Call Us" on service page)
- Rule: supports the primary CTA, never competes with it

**Tertiary / text CTA**
- Style: inline text link in orange, or small underlined link
- Purpose: navigation to related content (related articles, other services)
- Rule: used within content sections, card footers, breadcrumbs

### CTA rules by page type

| Page type | Primary CTA | Secondary CTA | Tertiary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Homepage | Book Online | View Services / Call | Service links, blog links |
| Service page | Book Your Assessment | Call / View pricing | Related services, blog links |
| Blog article | Book (inline or footer) | Related service link | Related articles |
| Profile page | Book with [Name] | View services | Credentials links |
| Community page | Contact for Collaboration | View event details | Past event links |
| Seminar (upcoming) | Register / Reserve Spot | Share event | Speaker profile |
| Resource (completed) | Download Guide | View related resources | Service links |
| Campaign page | Claim Offer / Book Now | Call / Share | Terms, FAQ |

### CTA anti-patterns
- Do not place two orange CTAs side by side leading to different destinations
- Do not use "Learn More" as a primary CTA (be specific about what happens next)
- Do not duplicate the same CTA in the same viewport unless separated by meaningful content
- Guide/resource pages must privilege download/view actions over booking
- Partner/community pages must privilege contact/collaboration actions over booking

## 7. Card and Section System

### Card anatomy
All cards share these base properties:
- White background
- 16-20px border-radius
- 1px border in light-gray `#E5E7EB`
- 24-30px internal padding
- Hover: -4px translateY, border-color to orange, box-shadow with orange glow `0 8px 30px rgba(255,107,53,0.1)`

### Card types

**Service card (homepage)**
- Two-column feature layout: text + image, alternating sides (`.reverse`)
- H3 title with orange highlight
- Short description (2-3 lines)
- Price/GESY indicator
- CTA: "Book Online" + "Learn More" link
- Grid: alternating left-right pattern

**Community/event card**
- Image area (220px height) with badge overlay (top-right)
- Title (H3), description, date/details
- Action area: button or link
- Badge: status indicator (Upcoming, Completed, Coming Soon)

**Blog promo card**
- Image thumbnail
- Category tag
- Title (H3), excerpt (2 lines max)
- Author + date meta
- "Read More" tertiary link

**Therapist card**
- Horizontal layout: circular photo (80px) + text
- Name (H3), title, key credentials
- CTA: "Book with [Name]"

**Pricing card**
- Vertical layout: label badge, price (large), description, features list
- Featured variant: orange border + orange label
- CTA button at bottom

**Partnership/case card**
- Partner logo or image
- Collaboration title
- Description of mutual value
- "Learn More" or "Contact for Collaboration" link

**Download/resource card**
- Navy gradient background (stands out from white cards)
- Icon (80px circle, orange 15% bg)
- Title with orange `<span>`
- Description
- Download CTA (orange button with icon)

**Speaker/expert block**
- Photo + credentials
- Relevant to seminar/resource context
- Links to full profile page

### Section patterns

**Alternating backgrounds:** white and off-white `#F8F9FA` sections prevent visual monotony. Every other section gets the off-white treatment.

**CTA sections:** navy gradient background `linear-gradient(135deg, #0A1628, #1a2940)`, white text, orange `<span>` on heading, orange CTA button. Used once per page near the bottom as a conversion anchor.

**Stats strip:** simple grid of stat-number + stat-label. Numbers in navy, bold. Labels in gray, regular weight.

**Callout block:** orange 8% opacity background, orange 4px left border, 20px padding. Used for important notes, disclaimers, key takeaways within article content.

## 8. Badge Taxonomy

### When badges help
Badges help when a page or card could be confused with another type. They set expectations before the user reads the content.

### When badges hurt
Badges hurt when they add noise without resolving ambiguity. A service page doesn't need a "Service" badge — the context is obvious.

### Badge catalog

| Badge | Style | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| **Free Resource** | Orange bg, white text, pill | Downloadable content that costs nothing |
| **Seminar Guide** | Navy bg, white text, pill | Materials from a specific seminar |
| **Completed** | Navy bg, white text, pill | Past event that still has value as content |
| **Upcoming** | Orange bg, white text, pill | Future event accepting registrations |
| **Coming Soon** | Navy bg, white text, pill | Announced but not yet open |
| **Partnership** | White/translucent bg, white border, pill | Collaboration with external organization |
| **GESY** | White/translucent bg, white text, pill | Service covered by national health system |
| **New** | Orange bg, white text, pill | Recently added content (remove after 30 days) |
| **Research** | Navy bg, white text, pill | Evidence-based or academic content |

### Badge styling rules
- All badges: pill shape (20-30px border-radius), 6-8px vertical / 16-20px horizontal padding
- Font: 0.8-0.85rem, weight 600-700, uppercase, 0.5px letter-spacing
- Position: top-right of card image (absolute, 16px offset) or inline in hero
- Maximum 2 badges per card. If you need 3, reconsider the card's clarity.

## 9. Typography and Hierarchy

### Brand book type system

| Role | Font | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Headings | **Hedvig Letters Sans** | Modern, confident, athletic — English only |
| Body | **Quattrocento Sans** | Elegant, readable, professional — English only |
| Multilingual fallback | **Noto Sans** | Neutral, covers Greek + all scripts |

### Practical implementation

The site uses the `system-ui` font stack as its base, which means the brand-book pairings are aspirational for English content and Noto Sans is the practical multilingual solution.

**Implementation strategy:**
1. Load Hedvig Letters Sans + Quattrocento Sans for English pages via Google Fonts
2. Load Noto Sans as the Greek fallback
3. Use `font-family` stacks that degrade gracefully:
   - Headings: `'Hedvig Letters Sans', 'Noto Sans', system-ui, sans-serif`
   - Body: `'Quattrocento Sans', 'Noto Sans', system-ui, sans-serif`
   - Greek pages: Noto Sans becomes primary since Hedvig/Quattrocento don't support Greek

### Hierarchy scale

| Level | Size | Weight | Line height | Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| H1 — Hero | 48-64px (3-4rem) | 800 | 1.1-1.15 | Page title, one per page |
| H2 — Section | 36-42px (2.2-2.6rem) | 800 | 1.2 | Section headers |
| H3 — Subsection | 24-28px (1.5-1.75rem) | 700 | 1.3 | Card titles, sub-sections |
| H4 — Label | 18-20px (1.1-1.25rem) | 700 | 1.4 | Small section headers |
| Body | 16-18px (1-1.125rem) | 400 | 1.6-1.8 | Paragraphs, descriptions |
| Caption | 12-14px (0.75-0.875rem) | 400 | 1.4 | Labels, meta text, badges |

### Rules
- Letter-spacing on H1/H2: -1px (tighter tracking for large text)
- Never use decorative or script fonts
- Maximum 2-3 font families per page load
- Greek pages must maintain the same hierarchy sizes and weights even when Noto Sans replaces the Latin fonts
- Bold (700-800) is reserved for headings and emphasis. Do not bold entire paragraphs.
- Orange `<span>` highlights: use on one word per heading maximum, always on the differentiating keyword

## 10. Color and Contrast Rules

### Brand palette

| Color | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Navy | `#0A1628` | Primary brand color, authority, backgrounds, headings |
| Orange | `#FF6B35` | CTA, emphasis, interactive elements, highlights |
| Midnight Blue | `#1E3A5F` | Secondary depth, gradients, supporting panels |
| Off-White | `#F8F9FA` | Alternate section backgrounds |
| Light Gray | `#E5E7EB` | Borders, dividers, card outlines |
| Success Green | `#22C55E` | Positive states, confirmations, checkmarks |
| Warning Amber | `#F59E0B` | Alerts, caution states |

### Color distribution (brand book ratio)
- **70% Navy** — authority base, dark sections, hero overlays, footer
- **20% Orange** — CTA buttons, highlights, badges, interactive accents
- **10% White/Off-White** — breathing room, content backgrounds, readability
- **Accents** — midnight blue for depth, green/amber for semantics only

### Application rules

**Backgrounds:**
- Page default: white
- Alternating sections: off-white `#F8F9FA`
- Hero sections: navy gradient + image overlay
- CTA sections: navy gradient `linear-gradient(135deg, #0A1628, #1a2940)`
- Download/resource cards: navy gradient
- Never: orange as a full-section background (too intense)

**CTA and interactive:**
- Primary buttons: orange `#FF6B35` background
- Hover state: darker orange `#e55a2b` + shadow
- Links within content: orange, underline on hover
- Active/focus states: orange outline or glow

**Text:**
- On dark backgrounds: white (headings), white 70-85% opacity (body)
- On light backgrounds: navy `#0A1628` (headings), `#212529` (body), `#6C757D` (secondary text)

**Borders and dividers:**
- Default: light-gray `#E5E7EB`
- Hover/active: orange `#FF6B35`
- Featured/highlighted card: orange 2px border

**Semantic colors:**
- Green `#22C55E`: success messages, "included" indicators, availability
- Amber `#F59E0B`: warnings, limited availability, important notes
- Never use green or amber for decorative purposes

### Do not
- Use soft pastel wellness colors (mint, lavender, blush)
- Create washed-out, low-contrast sections
- Introduce new colors not in this palette
- Use orange on orange (e.g., orange text on orange background)
- Use navy text on dark backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Default to white/blue clinical hospital aesthetic

## 11. Photography and Imagery

### Desired mood
- High contrast, dramatic lighting
- Dark gym/clinical environments
- Cinematic quality with strong shadows
- Muscle definition and effort emphasis
- Authentic moments of movement

### What to capture
- Real clinic and gym environment
- Movement and rehabilitation exercises in action
- Strength training and conditioning
- Athlete-clinician interaction (hands-on treatment, assessment)
- Equipment and treatment details
- Team photos in the actual facility

### What to avoid
- Stock photos and generic imagery
- Wellness/lifestyle aesthetics (yoga on beach, candles, smoothies)
- Overly clinical white/blue hospital style
- Soft pastel colors and spa-like imagery
- Flat, uninspired lighting
- Passive, non-athletic poses
- Smiling-at-camera corporate headshots (prefer action shots)

### Application by context

**Page banners/heroes:**
- Use real clinic/gym photos with navy gradient overlay
- Image opacity 30-40% behind overlay for text legibility
- Prefer wide-angle shots showing environment + movement

**Team/profile images:**
- Action shots preferred over posed portraits
- If posed, use gym/clinic setting, not studio backdrop
- Circular crop (80px) for inline therapist cards
- Larger format for profile pages

**Promo assets and social:**
- Bold headline over dark athletic image
- Navy background or image overlay with orange accents
- Max 3 lines headline, 2-3 lines body text
- Logo: bottom-right corner, small and subtle

**Presentation slides:**
- Full-bleed images where possible
- Same dark/dramatic treatment as website
- Never stretch or distort images
- Use consistent color overlay treatment across all slides in a deck

## 12. Graphic Elements

### Dynamic lines
- Horizontal lines in orange or navy indicating progress, movement, direction
- Use as section dividers or decorative emphasis under headings
- Orange line under H1 is a brand signature (visible in brand book cover)
- Keep lines thin (2-4px) and purposeful

### Directional arrows
- Orange arrows to guide attention or indicate progression
- Use in process steps, flow diagrams, exercise sequences
- Style: bold, simple, sharp — not rounded or playful

### Highlight boxes
- Three tiers from brand book:
  - **Orange box** — key stats, primary callouts
  - **Navy box** — important notes, secondary emphasis
  - **Midnight blue box** — supporting information
- All: strong border, bold label text, clear hierarchy

### Exercise markers / numbered steps
- Orange circle (50-60px) with white number for primary steps
- Navy circle for secondary steps
- Used in process sections, exercise sequences, how-it-works flows
- Keep numbering under 6 steps per sequence

### Body part indicators
- Target-style icons (concentric circles) in orange for treatment area focus
- Used in condition grids, exercise descriptions
- Available in orange (active/primary) and navy (secondary)

### Performance frames
- Bold border boxes (navy or orange outline) for highlighting key content
- Used for pricing cards, featured content, stats
- Orange border = featured/recommended

### Icon style
- Bold, filled icons with sharp edges (not rounded/playful)
- Colors: orange for primary, navy for secondary
- Background: circular, orange 15% opacity or navy solid
- Size: 40-80px depending on context
- Use for: service identifiers, value props, social proof icons, process step icons
- Consistent style across all pages — do not mix icon libraries

## 13. Liquid Glass System

The site currently uses a "liquid glass" effect across several interactive and structural UI elements. This is an established UI pattern on the site — not a mandatory brand law for all future work, but a documented convention that new pages may follow for visual consistency where appropriate.

### What liquid glass is
A translucent surface with background blur that creates depth and layering. The element appears to float above the content behind it while remaining visually connected to it.

### CSS pattern

```css
background: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.12);        /* or rgba(10, 22, 40, 0.6) for dark variant */
backdrop-filter: blur(10px) saturate(150%);
-webkit-backdrop-filter: blur(10px) saturate(150%);
border: 1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.15);
box-shadow:
  inset 0 1px 0 rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.15),    /* top inner highlight */
  inset 0 -1px 0 rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.1),          /* bottom inner shadow */
  0 4px 20px rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.15);             /* outer drop shadow */
```

### Where liquid glass is used

| Element | Blur | Saturate | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Header / nav bar** | 20px | 180% | White 70-40% opacity gradient, sticky, z-index 1000 |
| **Nav CTA buttons** | 10px | 150% | Primary (navy gradient) + secondary (white gradient) |
| **Back button** | 20px | 180% | Fixed position, white translucent bg |
| **Language switcher** | 10px | 150% | Both desktop and mobile variants |
| **Hero cards/panels** | 15px | 180% | Stats blocks, feature highlights on dark backgrounds |
| **Pricing badges** | 8px | 150% | GESY and price indicators in hero sections |
| **Mobile nav overlay** | 10px | 150% | Full-screen navigation on small screens |

### Two variants

**Light glass** (on dark backgrounds — heroes, navy sections):
- `background: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.08-0.15)`
- `border: 1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.15)`
- White text, white inner highlights
- Used for: hero badges, hero stats, feature cards on dark sections

**Dark glass** (on light backgrounds — content sections, header):
- `background: rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.7-0.9)` or `rgba(10, 22, 40, 0.6-0.8)`
- `border: 1px solid rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.5)` or `rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.1)`
- Navy/dark text
- Used for: header, nav buttons, floating UI elements

### Guidelines (when using glass)
- Always include both `backdrop-filter` and `-webkit-backdrop-filter` (Safari support)
- Blur values: 8-20px depending on element size (larger elements = more blur)
- Saturate: 150-180% to keep colors vivid through the blur
- Glass elements should have a visible border to define their edge
- Inset box-shadow (top highlight + bottom shadow) adds the "thickness" feel
- Glass panels must have sufficient contrast for text readability (test on both light and dark backgrounds)
- Reserve glass for UI chrome and interactive elements — not large content areas (card bodies, article text blocks)
- Do not mix glass with solid opaque backgrounds on the same element
- Hover states on glass buttons: increase opacity slightly, add translateY(-2px) lift
- New pages are not required to use glass — solid backgrounds with proper contrast are equally valid

### When not to use glass
- Blog article body text — readability takes priority
- Card bodies in content grids — use solid white with border
- Footer — solid background for stability
- Print or static exports — glass requires real-time rendering
- Decks/presentations — simulate the look with static translucent overlays, not actual CSS effects

## 14. Presentation / Deck Rules

### Slide system

**Cover slide**
- Full-bleed athletic image with navy gradient overlay
- RT logo centered or top-left
- Title: large, white, bold (H1 scale)
- "The Athlete's Physio" tagline
- Orange accent line under title
- Presenter name and date in caption size, white 70% opacity

**Section divider slide**
- Solid navy background
- Section number in orange (large)
- Section title in white, H2 scale
- Optional: subtle athletic image at 10-15% opacity

**Educational content slide**
- Off-white or white background
- H3 title in navy, left-aligned
- Body text in dark gray, 16-18px equivalent
- Orange highlight boxes for key points
- Generous whitespace — max 60% content area
- Relevant image or diagram on one side if needed

**Exercise / technique slide**
- Image dominant (60-70% of slide area)
- Navy overlay panel for text instructions
- Numbered steps with orange exercise markers
- Body part indicators if showing target area
- Keep text to 3-5 bullet points

**Data / stat slide**
- Navy background
- Large stat numbers in white or orange
- Supporting text in white 70% opacity
- Use highlight boxes for key figures
- Max 3-4 data points per slide — do not overcrowd

**Closing / contact slide**
- Navy background, centered layout
- RT logo
- "The Athlete's Physio" tagline in orange
- Contact details in white
- Social media handles
- Orange CTA: "Book Your Assessment" or relevant next step

### Deck rules

**Headlines:** Short, direct, action-oriented. "Build Ankle Stability in 4 Weeks" not "An Overview of Ankle Rehabilitation Approaches."

**Image treatment:** Same dark, dramatic, high-contrast photography as website. Apply navy overlay when text must sit on image. Never use clip art.

**Icon treatment:** Same bold, filled, sharp-edge icons as website. Orange on dark backgrounds, navy on light backgrounds. 

**Layout density:** Max 60% of slide area should contain content. 40% whitespace minimum. One key message per slide. If a slide needs scrolling, split it.

**Consistency with website:** Deck colors, fonts, and imagery should make someone think "this is the same brand as the website." Use the same palette, the same photography style, the same icon set. A slide should feel like a page of the website extracted into presentation format.

## 15. SEO Alignment

This section does not replace `docs/seo-guidelines.md`. It connects design/content decisions to search performance.

### Core principles

**One page, one clear intent.** Every page answers one question. The hero must reflect that intent. Do not build pages that try to rank for multiple unrelated queries.

**No mixed-intent hero.** If the hero says "Register for our seminar" but the page is now a resource download, the hero is lying. Update it.

**Metadata reflects visible purpose.** The `<title>` and `<meta description>` must match what the user sees above the fold. If the page hero says "Muay Thai Injury Prevention Guide" the title must not say "Muay Thai Seminar Registration."

**Page structure supports search intent.** Use correct heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3). Put the most important content first. Use semantic HTML (`<main>`, `<article>`, `<section>`).

**Internal CTA language reinforces canonical page role.** If this page's canonical role is "physiotherapy service page," then CTAs from other pages linking here should say "Learn about our physiotherapy" not "Check out this page."

**Refer to `docs/seo-guidelines.md` for:** URL rules, hreflang implementation, structured data requirements, sitemap rules, head tag templates, image optimization, internal linking strategy, release checklist.

## 16. Do / Don't

### Content and tone

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Make seminar pages feel like complete resources after the event ends | Leave dead registration CTAs on completed events |
| Make community pages partner-readable — a sports club director should understand the value | Write community pages only for internal team consumption |
| Frame every service around performance outcomes | Describe services as generic medical procedures |
| Use "return to sport" language for athletic audiences | Use "pain management" as the primary framing |
| Write like a coach explaining to an athlete | Write like a hospital brochure |
| Keep hero copy under 20 words | Write paragraph-length heroes |

### Visual and layout

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Use orange as intentional emphasis — CTAs, highlights, key moments | Spray orange everywhere until it loses meaning |
| Maintain high contrast between text and backgrounds | Create low-contrast sections where text is hard to read |
| Use dramatic athletic imagery with proper overlay treatment | Use stock photos of smiling people in lab coats |
| Keep card styles consistent across all page families | Invent new card patterns page-by-page |
| Use the navy gradient CTA section once per page as conversion anchor | Stack multiple navy CTA sections or skip it entirely |
| Alternate white and off-white section backgrounds | Use the same background for every section |

### Page structure

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Give every page one clear primary CTA | Let users guess what action to take |
| Put the most important information above the fold | Hide the value proposition below three sections of context |
| Make resource pages feel like part of the brand system | Let resource pages look like detached PDFs hosted on the site |
| Use badges to resolve ambiguity (Free Resource, Completed, Upcoming) | Slap badges on everything regardless of whether they help |
| Link resource/seminar pages back to relevant services | Create dead-end pages with no path to booking |

### Multilingual

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Maintain identical visual hierarchy between EN and EL pages | Let Greek pages look like lower-effort versions of English pages |
| Use Noto Sans as the Greek fallback that preserves heading weight and size | Accept broken or fallback-rendered Greek headings without checking |
| Translate CTA text with equivalent energy (not literal translation if it loses punch) | Machine-translate CTAs without reviewing tone |

### Decks and presentations

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Use the same dark/dramatic visual language as the website | Switch to a generic white corporate slide template |
| Keep one key message per slide | Cram multiple topics into one slide |
| Use orange for emphasis, navy for authority, white for breathing room | Introduce new colors "just for this deck" |
| End every deck with a clear CTA slide | End with a generic "Thank you" slide with no next step |
