Our Approach

We combine proven physiotherapy techniques tailored to your specific condition and goals.

Manual Therapy

Hands-on joint mobilisation and manipulation to restore movement, reduce stiffness, and relieve pain. Effective for spinal conditions, frozen shoulder, and post-surgical joints. Our therapists use graded techniques matched to your comfort level and stage of recovery.

Exercise-Based Rehabilitation

Individually prescribed exercises targeting strength, flexibility, and motor control. Every program is progressive — we adjust intensity and complexity as you improve. You receive a home exercise plan from day one so recovery continues between sessions.

Dry Needling

Targeted insertion of fine needles into myofascial trigger points to release deep muscle tension and reduce referred pain. Commonly used for chronic neck and back pain, tension headaches, and stubborn muscle tightness that doesn't respond to manual therapy alone.

Electrotherapy & Taping

TENS and other electrotherapy modalities to manage pain, support tissue healing, and reduce inflammation. Combined with kinesiology taping techniques that support joints, improve circulation, and assist muscle function between sessions. Used as part of a broader treatment plan — particularly helpful in acute injury and post-surgical phases.

Soft Tissue Therapy

Deep tissue massage, myofascial release, and trigger point therapy to address muscle tension, scar tissue adhesions, and restricted fascia. Integrated with movement work to ensure lasting results rather than temporary relief.

Patient Education & Self-Management

Understanding your condition is a critical part of recovery. We explain the science behind your pain, teach you strategies to manage flare-ups, and provide ergonomic and lifestyle advice to prevent recurrence. Our goal is to give you the tools to stay well independently.

Your First Visit

Know exactly what to expect when you walk through our door.

Duration: 45–60 Minutes

Your initial assessment is longer than follow-up sessions. We take the time to understand your full history, examine your condition thoroughly, and begin treatment in the same session — you won't leave without a clear plan.

What to Bring

Your GESY referral (if applicable), any medical reports or imaging (X-rays, MRI scans), and a list of current medications. Wear comfortable clothing that allows free movement — we may need to assess your range of motion and strength.

The Assessment

We start with a detailed conversation about your symptoms, medical history, and goals. Then a hands-on physical examination — testing movement, strength, joint function, and identifying the root cause of your problem, not just the symptoms.

Treatment Starts Day One

Unlike some clinics, we don't make you wait. After the assessment, we begin treatment immediately — whether that's manual therapy, targeted exercises, or pain-relief techniques. You leave your first session feeling better and knowing what comes next.

Your Recovery Roadmap

Before you leave, we outline a clear treatment plan: how many sessions we recommend, what we'll focus on, milestones to track progress, and realistic timelines. No vague promises — specific goals with measurable outcomes.

Home Exercise Program

You receive a personalised set of exercises to do between sessions. These are essential to your recovery — research shows patients who follow home programs recover significantly faster. We keep it simple, achievable, and relevant to your condition.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does GESY physiotherapy work?
Your doctor or specialist provides a referral for physiotherapy. Bring this to your appointment and GESY covers the treatment. Coverage may be full or partial depending on your plan.
How many sessions will I need?
This depends on your condition. Acute issues may resolve in 4-6 sessions, while chronic conditions may require a longer program. We set clear goals and reassess regularly so you know where you stand.
Do I need a doctor's referral?
For GESY: yes, you need a referral from your doctor or specialist. For private sessions: no referral needed — you can book directly and be seen within days.
What should I bring to my first session?
Bring any medical reports, imaging (X-rays, MRI), and your GESY referral if applicable. Wear comfortable clothing that allows movement. The first session includes a full assessment.
Can physiotherapy help with chronic conditions?
Yes. Evidence shows physiotherapy is effective for chronic pain, arthritis, and long-term conditions. We use a combination of exercise, manual therapy, and education to help you manage symptoms and improve quality of life.
What techniques do your physiotherapists use?
We use a combination of manual therapy (joint mobilisation, manipulation), exercise-based rehabilitation, dry needling, soft tissue therapy, electrotherapy, taping, and patient education. Your treatment plan is tailored to your condition — we select techniques based on the best available evidence for your specific diagnosis.
How long is each follow-up session?
Follow-up sessions are typically 30–45 minutes. The first assessment is longer at 45–60 minutes to allow for a thorough examination. Session length may vary depending on your condition and treatment plan.
Can I combine physiotherapy with your other services?
Absolutely. Many of our patients combine physiotherapy with clinical Pilates for core strengthening, sports massage for muscle recovery, or performance training for return to sport. Your physiotherapist can recommend the best combination for your goals.
Do you treat workplace and desk-related injuries?
Yes. We regularly treat conditions caused by prolonged sitting, poor posture, and repetitive strain — including neck pain, lower back pain, carpal tunnel syndrome, and tension headaches. Treatment includes hands-on therapy plus ergonomic advice to address the root cause.
What is the difference between GESY and private physiotherapy?
With GESY, you need a doctor's referral and treatment is covered (fully or partially) by the national health system. Private sessions (€35) require no referral — you can book directly and be seen within days. Both options receive the same quality of care and treatment from the same therapists.
Where is your physiotherapy clinic located?
Our clinic is at Tseriou 32, Strovolos, Nicosia. We're centrally located and easy to reach from Engomi, Aglantzia, Latsia, Makedonitissa, and Lakatamia. Street parking is available nearby.
