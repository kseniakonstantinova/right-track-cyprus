Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should I bring my child?
We treat children of all ages, from infants to teenagers. For scoliosis screening, we recommend assessment around age 10-12 when growth spurts begin. For developmental concerns, the earlier the better — early intervention leads to better outcomes.
How do you assess scoliosis?
We use the Adams forward bend test combined with a scoliometer for initial screening. If indicated, we coordinate with your paediatrician for imaging. Treatment includes specific exercises (Schroth method principles) and regular monitoring during growth.
Will my child need exercises at home?
Yes, home exercises are an essential part of treatment. We teach both the child and parents fun, manageable exercises that fit into daily routines. Consistency at home accelerates progress.
How often should we come?
This depends on the condition. Scoliosis monitoring may be every 3-6 months, while active treatment programs typically involve weekly or bi-weekly sessions. We set a clear schedule during the initial consultation.
Can I be present during sessions?
Absolutely. We encourage parent participation, especially for younger children. You'll learn the exercises and strategies so you can support your child's progress at home between sessions.
