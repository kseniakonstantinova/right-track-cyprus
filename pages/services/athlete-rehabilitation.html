What We Treat

Specialized rehabilitation for the injuries that keep athletes off the field, court, or mat.

ACL & Knee Injuries

ACL/MCL/PCL tears, meniscus injuries, patellofemoral pain. Criteria-based return to sport with objective strength and hop testing.

Ankle & Foot Injuries

Ankle sprains, Achilles tendinopathy, plantar fasciitis, stress fractures. Progressive loading and proprioceptive retraining.

Shoulder & Upper Limb

Dislocations, rotator cuff tears, labral injuries, throwing arm problems. From initial rehab to sport-specific strengthening.

Hamstring & Muscle Tears

Hamstring, quadriceps, and calf strains. Evidence-based loading protocols to rebuild strength and prevent re-injury.

Overuse & Tendon Injuries

Tendinopathies, shin splints, stress reactions. Load management strategies and progressive return to training plans.

Concussion Management

Graduated return-to-play protocols following concussion. Symptom monitoring and safe progression through activity stages.

How It Works

A structured, criteria-based approach — not a one-size-fits-all timeline.

1

Assessment

Comprehensive injury evaluation, movement analysis, and sport-specific demands assessment. We identify exactly where you are and set objective benchmarks for recovery.

2

Individualized treatment combining manual therapy, exercise prescription, and progressive loading. Sessions in our clinic and supervised gym work to rebuild strength.

3

Return to Sport

Sport-specific training, agility drills, and objective testing. You return when you meet the criteria — not when the calendar says so.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does return-to-sport rehabilitation take?
Timelines vary by injury. ACL reconstructions typically require 9-12 months, ankle sprains 6-12 weeks, and hamstring tears 4-12 weeks. At Right Track, return is criteria-based — we use objective strength, movement, and readiness tests rather than calendar dates to ensure you're truly ready.
Can I continue training during rehabilitation?
In most cases, yes. We modify your training program to maintain cardiovascular fitness and upper/lower body strength while protecting the injured area. Our on-site gym allows us to supervise your adapted training alongside your rehabilitation sessions.
What sports injuries do you treat?
We treat all sports injuries including ACL/MCL tears, ankle sprains, hamstring and quadriceps tears, shoulder dislocations, rotator cuff injuries, tennis and golfer's elbow, concussion protocols, and overuse injuries like shin splints and tendinopathies. We work with athletes from football, basketball, martial arts, padel, running, and many other sports.
Is athlete rehabilitation covered by GESY?
Yes. As GESY-registered physiotherapists (Antonis Petri: A2825, Charalambos Gregoriou: A3522), your sports injury treatment can be covered through the national health system. You'll need a referral from your doctor or orthopaedic surgeon. Private sessions are also available from €45 with no referral needed.
Do you work with professional sports teams?
Yes. Our lead clinician Antonis Petri works with competitive athletes and sports teams in Cyprus, and serves as Clinical Practice Supervisor at Frederick University. We collaborate with coaches and strength & conditioning professionals to ensure coordinated care throughout your return to sport.
